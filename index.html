I'm very interested in the theory and application of deep learning, which represents a promising ingredient towards artifical general intelligence. Theoretically, interesting research topics concern the understanding of deep neural networks (generalisation, capacity, optimization etc.), deep reinforcement learning, neural network architectures (Convolution, Autoencoder, LSTM/GRU, RBM etc.) and the combination with reinforcement learning. Practically, interesting applications are in computer vision (e.g. using geometric deep learning for bio-medical image analysis based on non-Euclidean data) and robotics.

Moreover, I have been writing kernels for several kaggle competitions, e.g. recently a Python script which implements a U-Net shaped convolutional neural network in TensorFlow for nuclei segmentation of the images provided by the 2018 Data Science Bowl.

My scientific background is in physics. I have obtained my diploma and PhD in theoretical high energy physics at the Johannes Gutenberg-University in Mainz, Germany, under the supervision of Prof. Dr. Matthias Neubert. In my research I have investigated Higgs and flavour physics in models with one warped extra dimension, the so-called Randall-Sundrum models. My work involved model building for elementary particles and the analytical calculation of Feynman loop-diagrams in a 5-dimensional warped space-time and the subsequent numerical analysis using Mathematica and C++. The predictions can then be tested in particle collision experiments like the Large Hadron Collider at CERN near Geneva. I have written peer-reviewed scientific papers, taught tutorials on advanced physics courses, and given talks about my research.

During my PhD I was awarded a membership of the Gutenberg Academy which is given to the best 25 young researcher of the entire Johannes-Gutenberg University Mainz. I finished my PhD with the highest grade possible (0.7, summa cum laude) and was awarded an endowed prize by "" for my thesis.
