I'm very interested in deep learning and deep reinforcement learning, which are promising ingredients towards general purpose intelligence. Theoretically, I am interested in representation learning (e.g. discover explanatory factors of the environment, deep generative adversarial models), in general properties of deep neural networks (e.g. expressivity, generalisation) and geometric deep learning (e.g. anisotropic convolution). Practically, I am interested in deep learning applications for computer vision (e.g. bio-medical image analysis, robotics), bio-informatics (e.g. protein structure prediction) and climate science (e.g. extreme weather forecasting). Moreover, I have been writing kernels for several kaggle competitions, e.g. recently Python code which implements a U-Net shaped convolutional neural network in TensorFlow for nuclei segmentation of the images provided by the 2018 Data Science Bowl.

My scientific background is in physics. I have obtained my diploma and PhD in theoretical high energy physics at the Johannes Gutenberg-University in Mainz, Germany, under the supervision of Prof. Dr. Matthias Neubert. In my research I have investigated Higgs and flavour physics in models with one warped extra dimension, the so-called Randall-Sundrum models. My work involved model building for elementary particles and the analytical calculation of Feynman loop-diagrams in a 5-dimensional warped space-time and the subsequent numerical analysis using Mathematica and C++. The predictions can then be tested in particle collision experiments like the Large Hadron Collider at CERN near Geneva. I have written peer-reviewed scientific papers, taught tutorials on advanced physics courses, and given talks about my research.

I finished my PhD with distinction (grade: 0.7, summa cum laude) and was awarded an endowed prize by Friends of Mainz University. During my PhD I was awarded a membership of the Gutenberg Academy which is given each year to the best 25 PhD students of the entire Johannes-Gutenberg University Mainz. My diploma thesis (grade: 1.0, very good) was awarded an endowed prize by the faculty Physics, Mathematics and Computer Science of the Johannes-Gutenberg University Mainz. During High School I won at the regional level the German youth science competition jugend forscht for writing a speech recognition software.
