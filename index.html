Currently, I'm very interested in understanding deep learning networks from a mathematical and physical viewpoint, see for instance this paper . Moreover, I have been writing kernels for several kaggle competitions, e.g. recently a Python script which implements a U-Net shaped convolutional neural network for nuclei segmentation of the images provides by the 2018 Data Science Bowl.

My scientific background is in physics. I have finished my PhD in theoretical elementary particle physics at the Johannes Gutenberg-University in Mainz, Germany. In my research I have investigated Higgs and flavour physics in models with one warped extra dimension, the so-called Randall-Sundrum models. My work involved the analytical calculation of Feynman loop-diagrams in a 5-dimensional warped space-time and the subsequent numerical analysis using Mathematica and C++. The predictions can then be tested in particle collision experiments like the Large Hadron Collider at CERN near Geneva.
